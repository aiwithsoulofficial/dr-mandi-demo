Script 1

Consultation Conversion Script

For use during skin consultations. The goal is to make the patient feel heard, recommend with confidence, and close with a clear next step.

Opening - Make Them Feel Safe

Say This

"Hi [Name], welcome - I'm Dr Mandi. Before we get into anything clinical, I'd love to hear from you. What's been on your mind about your skin? There's no right or wrong answer - just tell me in your own words what you'd like to change or improve."

Let them talk. Don't interrupt. Take notes on their exact words - you'll mirror this language back later. If they're nervous, acknowledge it: "It's completely normal to feel unsure - that's exactly what this consultation is for."

Discovery - Understand the Full Picture

Ask These

"How long has this been bothering you?"

"Have you tried anything before - skincare, treatments, anything at all?"

"On a scale of 1-10, how much does this affect your confidence day to day?"

"If I could wave a magic wand and change one thing about your skin, what would it be?"

The magic wand question reveals their real priority - which is often different from what they initially said. Listen for the emotional driver, not just the cosmetic concern.

Recommendation - Lead With Outcome

Say This

"Based on what you've told me, here's what I'd recommend. [Treatment Name] is going to help with [their exact words back to them]. Most of my patients who come in with a similar concern see [specific result] within [timeframe]."

"I'd suggest we start with [gateway treatment] - it's a beautiful entry point because [reason]. From there, we can build a plan that includes [secondary treatment] if you'd like to take it further."

Always lead with the outcome they want, then name the treatment. Never lead with treatment names - they don't mean anything to the patient. Use "we" language: "we'll start with..." - it positions you as a partner, not a salesperson.

Package Positioning

Say This

"For [primary concern], I'd normally recommend a course of [X] sessions. If you'd like, we can do a single session first so you can see how your skin responds - there's absolutely no pressure to commit to anything more until you're ready."

If they respond positively: "What a lot of my patients do is book 3 sessions upfront - that way you get the best results and we can plan your treatments properly. I can space them [X] weeks apart so it fits your schedule."

Close - Book the Next Step

Say This

"I'm really excited about what we can achieve for you. Let's get you booked in - would you prefer a weekday or weekend appointment?"

"Perfect. We'll take a [deposit amount] to secure your booking, and that goes straight towards your treatment. I'll send you a quick preparation guide so you know exactly what to expect."

Always offer two options (weekday or weekend), not yes or no. This assumes the booking and gives them control over timing. If they hesitate: "I completely understand - would it help if I sent you some information to look over at home? I can also send you some before-and-after photos from patients with similar concerns."
Script 2

Front of House Lead Follow-Up Script

For responding to new enquiries - phone calls, DMs, website forms, and walk-ins who didn't book. Speed matters: the first clinic to respond wins 78% of the time.

First Phone Call

Say This

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Dr Mandi Cosmetic Skin Clinic. I noticed you enquired about [treatment/concern] - I'd love to help you with that. Do you have a minute for a quick chat?"

If yes: "Wonderful. Can you tell me a little about what you're hoping to achieve? I can let you know the best options and get you booked in with Dr Mandi."

If they ask about price: "[Treatment] starts from $[price] per session. The best way to get an exact quote is with a consultation - Dr Mandi will assess your skin and recommend exactly what you need. There's no obligation at all. Would you like me to book that for you?"

Voicemail Script

Say This

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Dr Mandi Cosmetic Skin Clinic calling about your enquiry. I'd love to chat with you about [treatment] and help get you booked in. You can call us back on 0494 304 877, or just reply to the text I'm about to send you. Looking forward to speaking with you!"

Keep voicemails under 30 seconds. Warm and upbeat - not corporate. Always follow up with an SMS immediately after leaving a voicemail.

SMS Follow-Up (After No Answer)

Hi [Name]! This is [Your Name] from Dr Mandi Cosmetic Skin Clinic. I just tried to give you a call about your [treatment/concern] enquiry. Would you like me to book you in for a consultation with Dr Mandi? You can reply here or call us on 0494 304 877. We'd love to help! - [Your Name] x

Day 2 SMS (If No Reply)

Hi [Name], just following up from yesterday. I know life gets busy! If you're still interested in learning more about [treatment] with Dr Mandi, I'm here to help whenever suits you. No pressure at all - just let me know. - [Your Name] x

Day 5 Final Reach-Out

Hi [Name], I wanted to check in one last time about your [treatment] enquiry. Dr Mandi has some availability coming up this week if you'd like to book a consultation. If the timing isn't right, no worries at all - you're welcome to reach out whenever you're ready. We're here for you. - Dr Mandi Cosmetic Skin Clinic
After the Day 5 message, stop outreach. Add them to your email nurture list instead. Never make someone feel pressured - the follow-up sequence is about being helpful and available, not pushy. If they reply at any point, restart the conversation warmly as if no time has passed.
Bonus Script

Post-Treatment Review Request

Send 24 hours after treatment, when the patient is still feeling great about their experience.

Hi [Name]! It was lovely seeing you yesterday. We hope you're feeling great about your [treatment]. If you have a moment, we'd be so grateful if you could share your experience with a quick Google review - it really helps other women find us. Here's the link: [Google Review Link]. Thank you so much! - Dr Mandi x
Personalise the treatment name. Send from "Dr Mandi" not the clinic - it feels more personal. Track who has reviewed and don't ask the same patient twice.
